Innovation for autistic children (HCI project)

Jul 2015 – Dec 2015
Computer Science, University of Dhaka

Children with autism face a lot of difficulties in learning different things in school. As autistic children are slow learner, teachers make yearlong plans and put great effort to teach these children basic etiquette and social behavior. To make their learning process easier and more attractive, me along with a group of 8 members developed a computer based game for the children. By studying and analyzing about autistic children we came to know that technological devices and computer games seem interesting to the children. We found out that there is a very limited number of applications for teaching children about Bangladeshi culture and social behavior. So, we planned to build some puzzle games with attractive graphics and animations to teach children about Bangladeshi culture.

Key features:
  • Interactive features i.e. Q/A session, matching pictures etc. to teach children about Bangladeshi fruits.
  • Cheerful sounds and voices to motivate children while playing the game
  • Both English and Bengali interfaces have been used in the game .
Different phases of project implementation:
  • Need finding
  • Observation
  • Interview
  • Concept mapping
  • Story boarding
  • Sampling
  • Paper prototyping
  • Video prototyping
  • Implementation
